\section{Comparison with Related Work}\label{sec:disc:comparison}

Relative to classical \gls{elint} fingerprinter pipelines surveyed in \cref{sec:related:classical}, the proposed approach trades hand-engineered feature logic for a learned encoder, at the cost of data hunger, compute, and reduced transparency unless accompanied by diagnostics such as those in \cref{sec:exp:qualitative}.
Unlike supervised radar classifiers discussed in \cref{sec:related:dl_radar}, the training objective here does not assume a fixed label set for emitters or modes at train time; evaluation still uses reference labels where available, but they are not required by the learning rule itself.

Deep clustering methods in \cref{sec:related:deep_clustering} typically optimize a \emph{single} flat partition or require labels for each head.
This thesis instead targets two aligned partitions with a nesting interpretation (modes within emitters), which is closer in spirit to offline pulse-level grouping work that reasons about geometry and overlap in \gls{esm} data \parencite{bedoire2022offlineclustering} but uses gradient-based representation learning rather than hand-tuned density stages alone.
Where scores fall short of the best published supervised benchmarks, the gap is expected: those models exploit label information throughout training and often operate on curated single-emitter tracks, whereas the present setting stresses mixture windows and unsupervised objectives.
