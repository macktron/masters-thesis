\section{Problem Formulation}\label{sec:method:formulation}

\subsection*{Input}

A \emph{pulse train} is a finite, time-ordered sequence of \glspl{pdw}
\[
  \mat{X} = (\vect{x}_1,\ldots,\vect{x}_N),
  \qquad
  \vect{x}_n \in \R^{d},
\]
where each $\vect{x}_n$ encodes the measured parameters of one pulse (carrier frequency, amplitude, pulse width, time of arrival, and angle of arrival).
The length $N$ varies between trains.

\subsection*{Labels and induced partitions}

Each pulse $n \in \{1,\ldots,N\}$ carries two labels: an emitter identity $e_n \in \{1,\ldots,K_{\mathrm{em}}\}$ and an operating mode $m_n \in \{1,\ldots,M\}$.
The emitter symbol is unique only \emph{within the current train}: the same integer in a different train indexes an unrelated emitter, and $K_{\mathrm{em}}$ varies between trains.
The mode symbol is drawn from a finite \emph{global} catalogue of size $M$ that is shared across all trains, so $m_n = \ell$ identifies the same mode irrespective of the originating train.

These labels induce two partitions of the index set $\{1,\ldots,N\}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:method:emitter_partition}
  \mathcal{E}_k = \{\, n : e_n = k \,\},
  \qquad
  k \in \{1,\ldots,K_{\mathrm{em}}\},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:method:mode_partition}
  \mathcal{M}_\ell = \{\, n : m_n = \ell \,\},
  \qquad
  \ell \in \{1,\ldots,M\}.
\end{equation}
The first is defined only within a single train, the second is comparable across trains.
The two partitions are coupled: restricted to a fixed emitter $k$, the set $\{\, m_n : n \in \mathcal{E}_k \,\}$ is in general non-singleton, so $(m_n)_{n \in \mathcal{E}_k}$ may hop between several catalogue entries.

\subsection*{Dual embeddings and discrete outputs}

The parametric model maps $\mat{X}$ to two parallel embedding sequences,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:method:dual_embeddings}
  \mat{Z}^{\mathrm{em}} = (\vect{z}^{\mathrm{em}}_1,\ldots,\vect{z}^{\mathrm{em}}_N),
  \quad
  \vect{z}^{\mathrm{em}}_n \in \R^{p},
  \qquad
  \mat{Z}^{\mathrm{md}} = (\vect{z}^{\mathrm{md}}_1,\ldots,\vect{z}^{\mathrm{md}}_N),
  \quad
  \vect{z}^{\mathrm{md}}_n \in \R^{q}.
\end{equation}
The emitter branch $\mat{Z}^{\mathrm{em}}$ is trained so that, within each train, pulses with the same $e_n$ are close in $\R^{p}$ and pulses with different $e_n$ are pushed apart.
The mode branch $\mat{Z}^{\mathrm{md}}$ is trained analogously against $\{m_n\}$, but with positives and negatives drawn from the entire batch rather than from within a single train.

Discrete per-pulse outputs
\begin{equation}\label{eq:method:emitter_predictor}
  \hat{e}_n \in \{1,\ldots,\hat{K}_{\mathrm{em}}\},
  \qquad n \in \{1,\ldots,N\},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:method:mode_predictor}
  \hat{m}_n \in \{1,\ldots,\hat{M}\},
  \qquad n \in \{1,\ldots,N\},
\end{equation}
are obtained \emph{post hoc} by clustering $\{\vect{z}^{\mathrm{em}}_n\}$ within each train and $\{\vect{z}^{\mathrm{md}}_n\}$ across trains.
The cardinalities $\hat{K}_{\mathrm{em}}$ and $\hat{M}$ are determined by the clustering rather than fixed in advance; in particular $\hat{M}$ may exceed $M$ when modulation types absent from training are encountered at inference.

\subsection*{Learning objective}

Given a labelled collection of pulse trains, the goal is to fit the parameters of the map $\mat{X} \mapsto (\mat{Z}^{\mathrm{em}}, \mat{Z}^{\mathrm{md}})$ by minimising a sum of two supervised metric-learning losses that share their functional form but differ in scope: a within-train term acting on $\mat{Z}^{\mathrm{em}}$ with positives drawn from the same train, and a batch-wide term acting on $\mat{Z}^{\mathrm{md}}$ with positives drawn from anywhere in the batch.
On a previously unseen train, the inference task is to recover the partitions of \cref{eq:method:emitter_partition,eq:method:mode_partition} up to relabelling of the cluster symbols.
